AI automation works best when it starts with a repeated business action, not with a tool.

## Start With the Repetition

Good automation candidates usually have a familiar shape: copy data from one place to another, qualify an incoming lead, summarize a message, or notify the right person at the right time.

Before building anything, write down the trigger, the decision, and the final action. That gives the automation a clean boundary.

## Keep Humans in the Important Loop

For early systems, the best pattern is often draft first, send after review. This keeps speed high while protecting trust.

## Measure the Boring Wins

Track minutes saved, errors avoided, and response time improved. Those numbers are easier to explain than vague claims about intelligence.
